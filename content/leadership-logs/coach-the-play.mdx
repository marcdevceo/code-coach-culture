<h3 className="italic text-center mb-12">
  Numbers tell the story, but behavior writes it. Real coaching starts under the surface.
</h3>

<h3 className="mb-8">
  We live in a world obsessed with numbers. KPIs. SLAs. CSAT. AHT. But those metrics are just mirrors — not the drivers. If you want sustainable performance, don’t coach the number. Coach the play that leads to it.
</h3>

## The Problem with Metric-Based Coaching

<h3 className="mb-8">
  Coaching to the number is like yelling at the scoreboard instead of running a better play. The number is the result — but behavior is the cause. That’s where your coaching should live.
</h3>

<blockquote className="border-l-4 border-yellow-500 pl-4 italic text-xl mb-8">
  "You don’t fix the score by shouting at it — you fix it by adjusting the game plan."
</blockquote>

## Behavior Tells the Real Story

<h3 className="mb-8">
  Want to improve CSAT? AHT? Quality scores? Start by asking:
</h3>

<ul className="list-disc list-inside mb-8">
  <li>Is the agent rushing because they’re under pressure — or because they don’t know what good sounds like?</li>
  <li>Are they skipping steps because of laziness — or because the process doesn’t make sense?</li>
  <li>Do they sound robotic — or are they scared of saying the wrong thing?</li>
</ul>

<h3 className="mb-8">
  The answer isn’t always in the number — it’s in the behavior underneath. That’s the goal.
</h3>

## Culture Over Compliance

<h3 className="mb-8">
  I've watched agents transform when we stopped treating metrics like weapons and started treating them like windows. When you build a culture of curiosity and coaching — not punishment — performance rises naturally.
</h3>

<blockquote className="border-l-4 border-yellow-500 pl-4 italic text-xl mb-8">
  "Metrics don’t move people. Meaning does."
</blockquote>

## TL;DR for the Leaders in the Back

<ul className="list-disc list-inside mb-8">
  <li>The number is the symptom — not the cause.</li>
  <li>Behavior is where the shift begins.</li>
  <li>Coaching rooted in fear creates short-term compliance.</li>
  <li>Coaching rooted in curiosity builds long-term confidence.</li>
</ul>

<h3 className="font-semibold mt-12">
  Don’t chase the metric. Coach the moves that lead to it. That’s how you build both performance and trust.
</h3>

<h3 className="text-center text-md text-gray-700 mt-16">
  Keep building with clarity, confidence, and culture.
</h3>
